Why Visit Rome?

A A A

Rome is full of remains from its glorious past. You'll find a variety of sites from the famous Roman Colesseum and Forum to Roman baths and catacombs. Some ancient Rome sites can be visited for free but most charge admission. The city's architectural history reflects this tug-of-war between past and present. The Theatre of Marcellus is a Renaissance palace built on top of a Roman amphitheatre; Piazza Navona owes its elongated oval shape to the racetrack that once stood here. Nothing was wasted: spare bits of the Colosseum can be found in buildings all over the city.
